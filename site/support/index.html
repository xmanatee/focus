Common questions

Why does Focus Blocks need Screen Time permission?

Focus Blocks uses Apple's Screen Time framework (Family Controls) to hide the apps you choose during the schedules you set. The permission is required by iOS for any app that wants to apply shields to other apps. Selections and usage stay on your device.

Does my data leave the device?

No. The only thing that ever leaves the device is your schedules and selections, which are mirrored to your own iCloud account so that your setup is the same on your iPhone and iPad. Nothing goes to our servers — we don't have any. See the Privacy Policy for details.

How do I unblock an app temporarily?

Tap the shielded app on your home screen. The Focus Blocks shield appears with an "unlock briefly" action, which gives you short access without breaking the rest of your block.

I deleted the app and the shields stayed on. How do I clear them?

Go to Settings → Screen Time → See All App and Website Activity → Restrictions and clear any remaining entries. iOS keeps shields applied even after the controlling app is deleted; this is by design so your blocks survive a re-install.

Does Focus Blocks work for parental control?

No. Focus Blocks is self-directed — you set rules for yourself on your own device. It is not a parental-control product and does not manage other people's devices. For parental control, use Apple's built-in Screen Time with Family Sharing.

Which iOS versions are supported?

Focus Blocks requires iOS 18 or later.

Reporting a bug

If something behaves unexpectedly, the most useful thing you can include in an email:

  • What you were trying to do.
  • What happened instead.
  • iOS version (Settings → General → About → Software Version).
  • Any steps to reproduce, if you've found them.
