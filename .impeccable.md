# Fucus Design Context

## Users
iOS power-users fighting phone addiction / trying to do deep work. Tapped "Sign in" because Apple Screen Time alone isn't enough — they want something that makes the commitment **felt**, not a passive dashboard. They live in the same world as Opal / One Sec / Jomo users but are burned out on gamification and guilt.

## Brand Personality
**Bold. Confrontational. Editorial.** The app is a verdict, not a tool. When a session starts, the UI gets in your face — high contrast, heavy type, no decoration — then leaves you alone. It refuses to be pleasant-but-ignorable.

Three words: **committed, severe, exact.**

## Aesthetic Direction

### References
- **One Sec** — the interception moment (full-screen takeover, haptic weight). Fix its no-finish-line problem.
- **Dumbify / Blank Spaces** — typographic minimalism, refusal of icon-per-thing.
- **Redaction (the Titus Kaphar publication)** — editorial serif gravity.

### Anti-references (user-confirmed)
- **Freedom / clinical-corporate** — no flat blue, no enterprise chrome.
- **Apple Screen Time / Settings native** — no red-circle graphs, no rounded-blue-icon grid.

### Palette (dark-first, single accent)
OKLCH only. Warm-tinted neutrals, not pure black/white. One vivid signal hue — cadmium orange — used for commit moments and active state, nowhere else.

```
surface         oklch(14% 0.015 60)   near-black, warm cast
surface-raised  oklch(20% 0.015 60)   elevated cards
surface-sunken  oklch(10% 0.015 60)   wells
ink             oklch(96% 0.012 80)   paper-white
ink-muted       oklch(70% 0.015 60)   aged paper (captions)
ink-faint       oklch(45% 0.01 60)    disabled
signal          oklch(72% 0.19 47)    cadmium orange — ONE accent
signal-soft     oklch(80% 0.10 47)    muted orange (inline text highlight)
divider         oklch(25% 0.01 60)    hairline
danger          oklch(62% 0.21 25)    reserved; used rarely (abandon)
```

Light-mode mirror derived by flipping `surface` ↔ `ink`, same signal.

### Typography
SF Pro family only (no custom font dep this pass). Two moves distinguish it from the pack:

1. **Extreme weight contrast** — Ultralight next to Black. The timer is Ultralight at display size; headers are Black.
2. **Tabular nums** — all numerals use `fontVariant: ['tabular-nums']` so time reads aligned.

Scale (rem-ish in `fontSize`):
```
display-xl  64  ultralight (timer)
display-lg  44  black
display-md  32  black
h1          28  bold
h2          22  semibold
h3          18  semibold
body        16  regular
body-md     16  medium
caption     14  regular
label       12  medium, tracking wider, uppercase
```

### Motion
- Reanimated only. No spring-bounce. Exponential ease-out (`Easing.out(Easing.exp)`, 280–480 ms).
- Session start: serif timer scales from 0.92 → 1 + opacity 0 → 1 (420 ms).
- Session end: opacity 1 → 0, no scale (280 ms).
- Press: `scale: 0.97` on `pressIn`, restore on `pressOut`, 120 ms.
- No entrance animations for lists (they thrash on data churn).

### Haptics (`expo-haptics`)
- **Commit** (start session, save schedule) → `ImpactFeedbackStyle.Rigid`
- **Select** (day chip, duration preset) → `selectionAsync()`
- **Complete** (session ends successfully) → `NotificationFeedbackType.Success`
- **Abandon** (user aborts) → `ImpactFeedbackStyle.Soft` (never `.Error` — research flagged that as guilt design)

### Iconography
SF Symbols only via `expo-symbols`. Weight `regular`, size 20 inline / 24 in chrome / 28 in tab bar. No emoji. No PNG icons.

### Signature visual
The **countdown timer** is the app's face: SF Pro Ultralight numerals at 72pt centered on near-black, with a 1 px signal-colored progress bar under it depleting right-to-left. No ring. No widgets. No other UI on the screen during a session except a ghost "Abandon" at the bottom.

## Design Principles
1. **The word beats the icon.** If a label is enough, no glyph. No icon-per-section parade.
2. **One accent, applied with restraint.** Cadmium orange appears only on commit/active state — nowhere else. Its scarcity is its power.
3. **Editorial type, not product type.** Ultralight + Black weight contrast replaces color and illustration as the visual engine.
4. **Finish lines matter.** Every sensory moment (haptic, sound, animation) must have a defined end. No endless breathing loops.
5. **No red, no scolding, no guilt.** Abandonment is soft. Errors are short. The user is treated as the adult who asked for this.
6. **Near-black over pure black, paper-white over pure white.** Everything tints warm by 0.01–0.02 chroma for cohesion.
